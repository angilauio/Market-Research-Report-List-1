<p><p>The manganous fluoride market primarily includes two types: MnF2 and MnF3. Manganese(II) fluoride (MnF2) is the more common type, widely used in ceramics, glass, and as a chemical intermediary due to its stable properties. Manganese(III) fluoride (MnF3), though less prevalent, serves specialized applications in electronics and catalysis due to its higher oxidation state and reactivity. Both types play crucial roles in various industrial sectors, driving demand and innovation in materials and production techniques.</p></p>
